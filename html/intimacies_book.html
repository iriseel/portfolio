What does it mean to want, and make, something legible?erotophobia — the societal fear of freer-floating, nebulously defined erotic experienceThis brilliant article inspired me to realize a project dealing with interspecies intimacy, eroticism, desire, embodiment, queerness, and transhumanism. Diving deeper into the subject matter, I ended up reading, excerpting, and editing together a cross-genre anthology of pertinent texts. Entries include a training manual on how to become a cephalopod, an ecosex manifesto, poetry by Audre Lorde, a transcript of the youtube trailer to a documentary about the relationship between a NASA researcher and the dolphin she was studying, and academic essays such as an article by Donna Haraway on tentacular thinking.

The book is Japanese-folded and printed on semi-transparent paper, with images of mixed human and aquatic bodies and intimacies seeping out from within the folds onto the surface. Binding is hand-sewn with multiple thin, colored threads.
